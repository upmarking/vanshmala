
## 2024-05-18 - [React.memo in Recursive Structures]
**Learning:** In highly recursive visual components like `TreeNode` in this project, React.memo() optimization requires careful orchestration. When wrapping the component and its complex children (`SinglePersonCard`, `CoupleCard`, etc.) with `React.memo()`, you must also ensure that all handler functions passed down the tree (like `onAddRelative` and `onViewProfile`) are memoized using `useCallback()` at the topmost parent level (e.g., `FamilyTree.tsx` and `PublicTree.tsx`). Failing to memoize these handlers will cause the `React.memo()` wrapper to bail out at every level of the recursive tree, completely negating the optimization and leading to severe cascading re-renders when interacting with deeply nested nodes.
**Action:** When applying `React.memo()` to heavily recursive React components, aggressively audit all props passed down the tree, specifically ensuring that any callback functions are memoized with `useCallback()` at their origin.
